ABOUT ME

Early Career:

After leaving college at 18 years old I made the decison to not go to university as I was unsure what career path I wanted to take and did not wish to waste time and money on a course that would not lead to a future fulfilling career. I ended up working in a variety of jobs from retail and bar work to call centre and general admin work. None of these jobs appealed to me enough to want to make a career in, and having become more aware of the importance of being scientifically literate and the issues related to climate change and environmental pressures I then made the jump to University to start an undergradute degree in Environmental Science at the University of Plymouth.

Environmental Science at the University of Plymouth:

Before starting my undergradute degree I completed the University of Plymouth's Extended Science course, which in 2012 took place at the main university campus rather than at South Devon College. This year provided me the essentials to be able to confidently complete a 3 year science undergradute course which I managed to do with a First class honours. It was while studying for my Environmental Science course that I was introduced to Geographical Information Systems (GIS). I very quickly saw the benefits of learning about GIS which led to me undertaking a successful placement year at the United kingdom Hydrographic Office working in their Thematics team. As graduation approached I felt that so far I had barely scratched the surface of GIS software so to make myself more employable and to learn more about GIS I decided to embark on a Masters course at the University of Leeds.
